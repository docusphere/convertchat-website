# Competitive Analysis: Rasayel & LiveAgent vs ConvertChat

_Research date: 2026-07-04_

---

## 1. Rasayel — B2B WhatsApp Sales Platform

**Website:** https://www.rasayel.io/
**Category:** WhatsApp-only platform for high-touch B2B sales teams. Official Meta partner.

### Target Market

Mid-market B2B companies with 5+ person sales teams across:

- Technology & telecommunications
- Consulting
- Real estate
- Education
- Healthcare
- Automotive
- Retail

### Pricing

| Plan  | Cost        | Minimum                    |
| ----- | ----------- | -------------------------- |
| Start | $30/user/mo | 5 seats ($150/mo minimum)  |
| Grow  | $40/user/mo | 10 seats ($400/mo minimum) |

- 7-day free trial
- WhatsApp message costs (Meta fees) charged separately
- Campaigns included in all plans (no extra charge)

### Core Features

- Shared team inbox (WhatsApp only)
- No-code chatbot builder (lead qualification, FAQ, routing)
- AI agent "Cleo" — natural language understanding, real-time lead qualification, schedules calls, updates CRM, smart human handoff (not rule-based)
- WhatsApp broadcasts with pre-approved templates
- CRM integrations (HubSpot, Pipedrive, Salesforce, Zapier)
- Workflow automation & lead routing
- Agent performance analytics
- Internal notes & team assignment logic
- Mobile apps (iOS/Android)

### Weaknesses (from verified reviews)

- Reporting is poor — data/filters not useful for decision-making
- UI is cluttered, not user-friendly, steep learning curve
- Basic automation — can't handle complex workflows
- Limited template customization (reduces branding flexibility)
- Can't export data (tags, etc.)
- Expensive minimum seat requirements lock out small teams
- WhatsApp-only — no email, no multi-channel
- Managing multiple teams across multiple numbers is confusing
- Timer on conversations frustrates users
- Limited documentation

### What They Do Well

- Smooth onboarding experience
- Deep HubSpot/Pipedrive integrations
- Purpose-built for B2B sales collaboration
- Official Meta partner (compliance)
- Good for teams coordinating on long sales cycles

## 2. LiveAgent — Omnichannel Helpdesk

**Website:** https://www.liveagent.com/
**Category:** Traditional helpdesk/customer support software with WhatsApp as one of many channels.

### Target Market

Customer support teams across all industries. Broad horizontal play.

### Pricing

| Plan       | Cost         |
| ---------- | ------------ |
| Small      | $15/agent/mo |
| Medium     | $29/agent/mo |
| Large      | $49/agent/mo |
| Enterprise | $69/agent/mo |

- 30-day free trial, no credit card required
- WhatsApp integration costs extra
- Social integrations cost extra

### Core Features

- Unified inbox (email, live chat, phone, social, WhatsApp, Viber, Telegram)
- Ticketing system with automation rules
- Built-in call center (VoIP) with recording
- Knowledge base builder
- AI chatbot (basic, rule-based, 100+ languages)
- 200+ integrations
- WordPress plugin
- Mobile apps (iOS/Android)

### WhatsApp-Specific Capabilities

- WhatsApp messages converted into helpdesk tickets
- Connect via 360dialog or Twilio
- Reply freely within 24h window
- After 24h, only template messages allowed
- Works with tags, notes, split tickets, canned messages
- No broadcast/campaign capability
- No proactive outbound messaging

### Weaknesses (from verified reviews)

- WhatsApp integration is limited and costs extra
- Cannot use WhatsApp proactively (no campaigns, no broadcasts)
- AI is basic and rule-based — can't learn from context or past tickets
- UI feels clunky and outdated, especially settings
- Mobile app is significantly worse than desktop
- Cost adds up with extras (social, AI, custom branding)
- Mixed customer support quality
- Not built for sales — purely reactive/support oriented

### What They Do Well

- True omnichannel (deepest channel coverage)
- Affordable entry point ($15/agent)
- Mature ticketing system
- Built-in call center
- 30-day trial with full features
- Good for traditional support operations

## 4. ConvertChat's Differentiators

### 1. Email-to-WhatsApp Conversion Funnel

Neither competitor does this. Rasayel assumes you already have WhatsApp contacts. LiveAgent doesn't do outbound at all. ConvertChat solves the "how do I GET WhatsApp contacts?" problem.

Flow: Email campaign (product + WA CTA) -> Click -> WhatsApp opt-in -> Future WA campaigns

### 2. Solo/Micro-Business Friendly

Rasayel forces 5-seat minimums ($150/mo). LiveAgent charges per agent. ConvertChat serves the solo operator who has 1,000 contacts and needs 8-10 clients. No team features required.

### 3. Non-Technical Simplicity

Rasayel has a steep learning curve and cluttered UI. LiveAgent is enterprise-complexity. ConvertChat targets "4 clicks to send a campaign" for non-technical users.

### 4. Full Acquisition Pipeline

Cold email -> WhatsApp opt-in -> WhatsApp campaigns -> AI agent -> Repeat customers. End-to-end in one tool. Competitors only do one slice.

### 5. Compliant by Design

Unlike gray-area bulk tools (ConvertChats.com), ConvertChat uses official WhatsApp Business API. Unlike LiveAgent's limited WhatsApp bolt-on, ConvertChat builds WhatsApp-native workflows from the ground up.

### 6. AI Agent for Solo Operators

Rasayel's AI ("Cleo") is built for B2B sales teams — it qualifies enterprise leads, schedules calls, updates CRM, and alerts reps. It's a team assistant. LiveAgent's AI is rule-based and can't learn. ConvertChat's AI agent serves solo operators — handling conversations 24/7 when there's literally no one else to answer, acting as a virtual employee rather than a team assistant.

## 5. Strategic Positioning

### Positioning Statement

"ConvertChat turns your email list into WhatsApp customers — the simplest way for small businesses to build a direct line to buyers."

### Who We Win Against Each Competitor

**vs Rasayel:** Solo operators and micro-businesses priced out by 5-seat minimums. Businesses that don't have WhatsApp contacts yet (only email lists). Non-technical users frustrated by Rasayel's steep learning curve.

**vs LiveAgent:** Anyone who needs outbound/proactive messaging (not just support). Businesses that want WhatsApp as a sales channel, not just a support ticket source. Users who want real AI, not rule-based chatbots.
