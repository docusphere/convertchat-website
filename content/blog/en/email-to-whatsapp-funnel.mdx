## Why your email list is an untapped WhatsApp asset

Most B2B companies sit on email lists of hundreds or thousands of contacts. Trade show scanners, LinkedIn exports, CRM databases, webinar registrations. They have emails. What they usually don't have is phone numbers. And WhatsApp requires a phone number to start a conversation.

That gap is where the email-to-WhatsApp funnel lives. Instead of treating email and WhatsApp as separate channels (which is what every platform on the market does today), you use email as the bridge that captures the phone number and moves the contact into a WhatsApp conversation where engagement rates are 10x higher.

The idea is simple. Email is cheap, scalable, and you already have the list. WhatsApp is where people actually reply. Connect the two and you get a pipeline that costs almost nothing to fill and converts at rates your email campaigns never will.

I've been running this funnel for B2B clients at ConvertChat for months. The numbers consistently outperform both pure cold email and ads-to-WhatsApp. This article breaks down exactly how it works, what the real numbers look like, and why nobody else in the market has built tooling for it.

## Email vs WhatsApp: the numbers side by side

Before we get into the funnel mechanics, let's look at the raw channel performance. These are industry benchmarks from published sources, not ConvertChat data.

| Metric                      | Email                                       | WhatsApp                            |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Open rate                   | ~20% (real, post-Apple MPP)                 | 95-98% (measurable ~80%)            |
| Click-through rate          | 1-4%                                        | 15-45% (up to 60% with CTA buttons) |
| Reply/response rate         | Less than 1% (marketing), 5-10% (B2B sales) | 45-55%                              |
| Conversion rate (campaigns) | 1-5%                                        | 10-25% (automated flows)            |
| ROI per $1 spent            | $36-40 (Litmus/Statista)                    | $26-58 by vertical (Kanal, 2026)    |

A note on email open rates: since Apple Mail Privacy Protection launched in September 2021, email open rates are systematically inflated. Apple pre-fetches all email content, registering an "open" regardless of whether the person actually read the message. Reported email open rates now show 39-43%, but the real engagement open rate is closer to 20% once you strip out the Apple phantom opens.

The interpretation is straightforward. Email is excellent at **reach**. It's cheap, scalable, and you can send to thousands for pennies. But it's weak at **engagement**. Most emails get ignored, and the ones that get opened rarely generate a reply.

WhatsApp is the opposite. It's excellent at engagement (45-55% reply rates, 10-25% conversion rates in automated flows), but it requires something email doesn't: a phone number. You can't start a WhatsApp conversation with an email address.

The funnel combines both strengths. Email handles the reach. WhatsApp handles the conversion. The email-to-WhatsApp link is the bridge between the two.

## Three entry points to WhatsApp (and when to use each)

WhatsApp conversations have to start somewhere. There are three main entry points, and each one fits a different scenario.

| Scenario                                               | What you have    | Best entry point                  |
| ------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Cold leads from trade databases, LinkedIn, directories | Email only       | Email to WhatsApp                 |
| Existing customers                                     | Phone number     | WhatsApp directly                 |
| Anonymous website visitors                             | Nothing          | Website widget or ads to WhatsApp |
| Social media audience                                  | Nothing specific | Ads to WhatsApp (CTWA)            |

Let's compare the economics of each.

**Email to WhatsApp.** Cost per email sent: approximately $0.001 (Resend, AWS SES, similar transactional providers). You're working from your own curated list with exact targeting. This is the best entry point for B2B niche outbound where you already have email contacts but not phone numbers.

**Ads to WhatsApp (CTWA).** Cost per conversation started: $1-10 in developed markets, depending on targeting and vertical. You're using Meta's ad targeting to reach new audiences. Click-to-WhatsApp ads are powerful for D2C and broad consumer audiences. But for a B2B wholesaler with 2,000 contacts from a trade show, spending $5-10 per conversation when you could have reached them for $0.001 via email doesn't make sense.

**Website to WhatsApp.** Requires existing inbound traffic. If you already have strong SEO or paid traffic driving visitors to your site, a WhatsApp widget captures conversations from that traffic. But you need the traffic first, which makes this a secondary channel, not a primary acquisition tool.

The point is not that email-to-WhatsApp is universally better. It's specifically better for cold B2B outreach where you have email lists but not phone numbers. That covers every wholesaler, distributor, and manufacturer sitting on a trade show contact list, a LinkedIn export, or a CRM full of emails that haven't converted.

:::callout tip
The 72-hour window for click-to-WhatsApp ads (CTWA) remains free of charge. Meta doesn't bill for conversations that start from an ad click within that window. But you still pay for the ad itself. With the email-to-WhatsApp funnel, the entry cost is just the email send.
:::

## How the email-to-WhatsApp funnel works

Here's the step-by-step flow.

**Step 1: Send an email campaign with a WhatsApp CTA.** The email contains a clear call to action: "Message us on WhatsApp" or "Get your quote on WhatsApp." The link uses a wa.me URL pre-filled with a message, so the recipient lands directly in a WhatsApp conversation with your business.

**Step 2: The click captures the phone number.** When the recipient clicks through and sends the pre-filled message, you now have their phone number. They've initiated the conversation, which also gives you implicit consent under Meta's rules (customer-initiated conversations). This is the consent bridge.

**Step 3: The conversation starts on WhatsApp.** The contact is now in WhatsApp, where open rates are 95%+ and reply rates are 45-55%. Every subsequent message you send in this channel will actually be seen and read.

**Step 4: An AI agent qualifies the lead automatically.** The AI agent responds within seconds, asks qualifying questions (budget, timeline, specific product interest), and routes the lead based on the answers. No human needed for the initial qualification.

**Step 5: Qualified leads get routed to sales.** Only the leads that pass qualification criteria land on a salesperson's desk. The salesperson gets a warm, pre-qualified lead with full conversation context instead of a cold email reply that says "send me info."

This is a generalized model based on industry benchmarks, not a specific customer's data. Your numbers will vary based on list quality, targeting, and offer relevance.

| Step                   | Channel           | Expected rate         | From 500 emails |
| ---------------------- | ----------------- | --------------------- | --------------- |
| Emails sent            | Email             | —                     | 500             |
| Opened                 | Email             | ~20%                  | 100             |

Result: approximately 3 qualified leads from 500 emails, with zero manual qualification work.

Compare that to pure cold email. At a 5-10% reply rate on B2B sales emails, you'd get 5-10 replies from 500 emails. But each reply needs manual reading, manual qualifying, manual follow-up over days or weeks. And most of those replies will be "not interested" or "send me more info" (which is code for "I'll forget about this in 24 hours").

The email-to-WhatsApp funnel produces fewer leads, but better ones. Leads that are already in a conversation, already qualified, and already expecting a next step.

## Why nobody else is doing this

There are three structural reasons why the email-to-WhatsApp funnel doesn't exist in the market today.

**1. WhatsApp platforms don't have email.** Wati, ManyChat, FunnelChat, and similar tools are WhatsApp-native. Their entire acquisition model is built around ads-to-WhatsApp (CTWA) or website widgets. They have no email sending capability, so they can't build the bridge even if they wanted to.

**2. Email platforms treat WhatsApp as a separate channel.** Mailchimp, Brevo, ActiveCampaign. These platforms have added WhatsApp integrations, but they treat it as a parallel channel, not as a funnel continuation. You can send a WhatsApp blast OR an email blast. But there's no workflow that says "send email, capture phone from click, continue on WhatsApp, qualify with AI."

**3. No tooling existed to make the handoff smooth.** The technical challenge isn't trivial. You need to track the email click, capture the phone number from the WhatsApp conversation initiation, match it back to the email contact, and start an automated qualification flow. All within seconds. That requires integration between email infrastructure, the WhatsApp Business API, and an AI agent layer. Nobody had built that stack.

Speed matters too. Research from InsideSales.com and the Harvard Business Review shows that leads contacted within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to qualify than those contacted after 30 minutes. The email-to-WhatsApp funnel gets you from "email opened" to "WhatsApp conversation with AI agent" in under 60 seconds.

## FAQ

### Do I need my contacts' phone numbers before starting?

No. That's the entire point of this funnel. You start with email addresses only. The email campaign contains a WhatsApp CTA link. When the recipient clicks through and initiates the WhatsApp conversation, you capture their phone number at that moment. You go from email-only contacts to WhatsApp conversations without needing to collect phone numbers separately.

### Is this GDPR and LSSI-CE compliant?

Yes, when set up correctly. Your email list needs valid consent for commercial communications (which it should already have under GDPR Article 6(1)(a) and LSSI-CE Article 21). The WhatsApp conversation is customer-initiated, meaning the contact clicked through and sent the first message, so it meets Meta's consent requirements.
