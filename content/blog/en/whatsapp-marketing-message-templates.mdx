## Most template lists set you up to fail

Search for "WhatsApp marketing message templates" and you'll find dozens of listicles. Most share the same problem: the templates look reasonable but get rejected by Meta's review, or they get approved and then paused within days because they trigger blocks and low read rates.

Every marketing template includes an opt-out line. Six of the templates include chat mockups showing how the conversation plays out after the customer receives them.

If you're sending through the [WhatsApp Business API](/blog/whatsapp-business-api-pricing), you need approved templates for every outbound message. There is no way around this.

## How template approval works

Every outbound message you send through the WhatsApp Business API starts as a template. You write it, submit it through your provider or Meta Business Manager, and Meta reviews it before you can use it.

Three categories exist: **marketing** (promotions, offers, re-engagement), **utility** (order confirmations, shipping updates, appointment reminders), and **authentication** (OTPs, verification codes). The category matters because it determines price. In Spain, a marketing message costs €0.0585 per delivered message vs. €0.0166 for utility.

Since April 2025, Meta auto-recategorizes templates. You can submit a template as utility, but if Meta's classifier decides it's promotional, it gets approved as marketing and billed at the marketing rate. The category you pick is a suggestion, not a guarantee.

Review takes up to 24 hours. In practice, most templates are approved or rejected within minutes. Every variable in your template needs an example value at submission time.

Your WABA can hold up to 250 templates before Meta Business Verification, and 6,000 after.

## The formatting rules that get templates rejected

This is the part most template lists skip. Meta's review system auto-rejects templates for structural reasons before a human ever looks at the content. Know these rules and you won't waste days resubmitting.

- **No dangling parameters.** Your template body cannot start or end with a variable like {{1}}. There must be fixed text before the first variable and after the last one.
- **No adjacent variables.** {{1}} {{2}} back to back with only whitespace between them gets rejected. Put fixed text between every variable.
- **Sequential numbering.** Positional variables must go {{1}}, {{2}}, {{3}} in order. No skipping. Named parameters like {{first_name}} are also supported.
- **Too many variables.** If your template is mostly variables with little fixed text, it gets auto-rejected. Keep the ratio weighted toward actual copy.
- **No URL shorteners.** bit.ly, tinyurl, ow.ly. All rejected. Use full URLs on your own domain.
- **No duplicate body text.** If the body text matches an existing template on your account word for word, it gets rejected as a duplicate. Change the copy, don't just swap variables.
- **Character limits.** Header: 60 characters. Body: 1,024 characters. Footer: 60 characters. Button labels: 25 characters.
- **Button constraints.** Up to 10 quick-reply buttons, 2 URL buttons, 1 phone button. Coupon copy-code buttons: 15 characters max.

:::callout warning
URL shorteners are the single most common reason we see templates rejected on first submission. If your link goes through bit.ly or any redirect service, rewrite it as a full URL on your domain before submitting.
:::

## Personalization that protects your quality rating

Templates with the recipient's name and a specific product or context reference consistently outperform generic blasts. Open rates are only part of it. Personalized messages get fewer blocks, and fewer blocks keep your quality rating healthy.

Meta tracks blocks and reports per template. Enough negative signals and your template gets paused or disabled (more on that below). Personalization protects your quality rating as much as it lifts your response rates.

Include an opt-out line in your marketing templates. Something like "Reply STOP to opt out." Meta increasingly treats this as expected in marketing templates during review, and it reduces blocks from recipients who'd otherwise report you.

## Why approved templates get paused anyway

Getting a template approved is step one. Keeping it active is step two.

Meta runs a per-template quality system. Every template has its own quality score based on recipient reactions: blocks, reports, and read rates. When enough recipients block your message or your read rate drops too low, Meta intervenes.

The escalation works like this:

1. **First offense:** 3-hour pause. Your template can't be sent for 3 hours.
2. **Second offense:** 6-hour pause.
3. **Third offense:** template disabled permanently.

This is per template, not per account. One bad template won't take down your other templates, but it will take itself out of rotation.

Practical advice to keep templates active:

- Send to opted-in contacts only. Purchased lists generate blocks.
- Segment your audience. A template about auto parts shouldn't go to your restaurant supply buyers.
- Don't blast your entire list with one template on day one. Ramp up and monitor quality in Meta Business Manager.
- Rotate templates. Even good copy fatigues an audience when they see it five times.

## Common rejection reasons and fixes

| Rejection reason | Fix |
| --- | --- |
| Variable at start or end of body | Add fixed text before {{1}} and after the last variable |
| Adjacent variables ({{1}} {{2}}) | Insert descriptive text between every variable |
| Variables not sequential | Renumber to {{1}}, {{2}}, {{3}} in order |
| Too many variables vs. fixed text | Add more actual copy, reduce variable count |
| URL shortener detected | Replace with full URL on your own domain |
| Duplicate body text | Rewrite the copy so it doesn't match an existing template |
| Header exceeds 60 characters | Shorten. Headers are meant to be brief |
| Body exceeds 1,024 characters | Trim copy or split into two templates |
| Button label exceeds 25 characters | Shorten the button text |
| Missing example values | Provide realistic example content for every variable |

:::callout tip
If your template keeps getting rejected and you can't figure out why, check for invisible Unicode characters. Copy-pasting from Google Docs or Notion sometimes introduces zero-width spaces that trip Meta's parser.
:::

## Frequently asked questions

### How long does template approval take?

Most templates are reviewed and approved within minutes. Meta's official timeline is up to 24 hours. Complex templates or templates in certain languages occasionally take longer. If your template is stuck in "Pending" for more than 24 hours, re-submit it. Sometimes submissions get stuck in a queue and a fresh submission processes faster.

### Why was my template rejected even though it looks fine?

The most common invisible causes: a variable at the very start or end of the body text, adjacent variables with no fixed text between them, a URL shortener embedded in the copy, or duplicate body text matching another template on your WABA. Check the formatting rules section above. Meta's rejection messages are often generic ("content policy violation"), so you have to work through the checklist yourself.

### Can I edit an approved template?

Editing a template sends it back through Meta's review process. During review, the template is unavailable. If you need the template live continuously, create a new version as a separate template, get it approved, then swap it into your campaigns.

### What's the difference between marketing and utility templates?

Marketing templates are promotional: offers, re-engagement, product launches. Utility templates are transactional: order confirmations, shipping updates, appointment reminders. The cost difference is significant. In Spain, marketing is €0.0585 per message vs. €0.0166 for utility. Since April 2025, Meta auto-recategorizes templates that don't match their submitted category. Submit order confirmations as utility. Submit promotions as marketing.

### Do I need opt-in to send these templates?

Yes. WhatsApp requires that contacts have opted in to receive messages from your business on WhatsApp specifically. A general marketing consent or having their phone number is not enough. Send to contacts who haven't opted in and you'll accumulate blocks, your quality rating will drop, and Meta can restrict or ban your number. See Meta's [opt-in policy](https://business.whatsapp.com/policy) for details.
